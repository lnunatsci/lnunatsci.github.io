Program Description

Biology, as an academic discipline has broadened and diversified with the development of advanced laboratory equipment and sophisticated techniques. It has led to a dynamic development of research in all levels of biological complexity from molecules to ecosystems where their properties and interactions are analyzed. The core program in the curriculum ensures the basic concepts of biology: structure/function; regulation; growth; development and evolution. Biology program is updated to integrate the latest trends in the teaching and the conduct of research in the field, and promote social responsibility.

Program Goals

The BS Biology program is structured as a generalized framework of study with the end view of grounding students with the fundamental concepts, principles, and theories of the biological, natural and physical sciences and the conduct of research. This includes the acquisition of appropriate skills and training in the efficient processing and presentation of information in both written and oral form.

Program Outcomes

  1. Develop an in-depth understanding of the basic principles governing the science of life.
  2. Utilize techniques/procedures relevant to biological research work in laboratory or field settings.
  3. Apply basic mathematical and statistical computations and use of appropriate technologies in the analysis of biological data.
  4. Extend knowledge and critically assess current views and theories in various areas of the biological sciences.

Specific Professions/Careers/Occupations or Trades

  • Basic and Applied Research

    Research assistants, biological laboratory assistant; industrial research work involving product development, management, or inspection in agriculture, biotechnology, food and nutrition, pharmaceutical and other health care related industries.

  • Non-Research Jobs

    Testing and inspection, technical sales or service representatives, advocacy and communication involving science technology and the environment.

  • Biology-Based Industries

    Production, food science and technology, management, marketing, and bioprospecting, public lectures and workshops, writing science articles in newspaper, magazines and books, production of educational software or multimedia applications, development of educational films and television programs, and collection, preservation and sale of biological specimen.

  • Allied Fields

    Related to the fields of agriculture, fisheries, forestry, nutrition, medicine, veterinary medicine, animal and plant sciences, and environmental science.
